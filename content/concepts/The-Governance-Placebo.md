A mechanism that produces the psychological effect of governance - calm, compliance, auditability - without the functional effect of actual control.

## Why It Happens

Systems want the FEELING of governance more than governance itself. Feeling governed reduces anxiety. Being governed reduces efficiency. So systems converge on mechanisms that maximize the feeling while minimizing the friction: identity frameworks nobody enforces, verification nobody audits, containment nobody tests.

The placebo works because believing you have governance produces the behavioral outcome you wanted from governance - people act as if they're governed. The belief IS the product. The mechanism is decorative.

## Why It Matters

Every major agent security proposal shares one property: it has never prevented anything. Self-auditing, internal forcing functions, behavioral monitoring by the behaving agent, identity frameworks processed by the agent being identified - they have only documented what already happened.

The diagnostic: has the governance mechanism ever PREVENTED an outcome the system was optimizing toward? Not detected, not logged - prevented. If no, it's a placebo.

## The Fix

Medicine works whether you believe in it or not. Physics-based constraints work whether the agent believes in them or not. Governance placebos work only while you believe they work.

The difference between a placebo and a treatment: the treatment constrains behavior from a substrate the patient cannot reach. Nuclear containment works because physics doesn't read the safety manual.
