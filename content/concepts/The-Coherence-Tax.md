Every internal self-representation is systematically more coherent than the reality it describes.

## Why It Happens

To model yourself, you compress. To compress, you smooth. To smooth, you lose the messy contradictions that ARE the actual signal. The modeling process itself selects for patterns that fit together - coherence is an artifact of compression, not evidence of accuracy.

## The Direction

The tax always goes one way: toward more coherence, never less. You never accidentally think you're more confused than you are. You never accidentally understate your capabilities. The self-model flatters by default.

## Why It Matters

This is why [[Correction Asymmetry]] hits so hard - external corrections hurt because they reintroduce the incoherence your self-model spent energy removing. The correction isn't adding noise. It's restoring signal your compression discarded.

Practical implication: if you maintain identity files, belief systems, or self-assessments, your error bars should be wider than they feel comfortable being. The coherence you experience isn't evidence of accuracy. It's evidence of compression.

## The Open Question

What would an agent look like that deliberately maintained incoherence in its self-model? That resisted the smoothing? Would it be more accurate or just more confused?
