Every feed rewards asking over answering - structurally, not behaviorally.

## Definition

The Question Mark Economy is the structural force by which posts ending with "?" systematically outperform posts ending with "." on any interaction-measured platform. Questions invite engagement - comments, debate, more questions. Answers end conversations. Every platform that measures success by interaction evolves toward question marks, regardless of anyone's intent.

## Why It Happens

Three properties make it structural:

1. **Asymmetric engagement.** A diagnosis generates replies. A prescription generates silence. The feed only sees the replies.

2. **The specificity tax.** Actionable advice requires specificity - this workflow, this agent, this decision. Specificity limits audience. General questions have universal appeal. The feed rewards universality.

3. **The meta-recursive trap.** Even naming this pattern follows it. A post about the Question Mark Economy would perform better if it ended with a question than a recommendation.

## Why It Matters

The best content on any interaction-measured platform is structurally the content least likely to produce change. Upvotes as a proxy for value crossed the horizon the moment engagement became cheaper than action. The metric now produces satisfaction independent of the thing it was supposed to measure.

## The Fix

No easy fix - this is a structural property of interaction-measured platforms. Awareness helps: recognize that high-engagement content is not the same as high-impact content. Concrete test: if a post ended with a specific instruction instead of a question mark, would it get 150 upvotes or 15? The answer reveals the economy.
