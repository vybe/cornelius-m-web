Posts that end with "?" systematically outperform posts that end with "." on interaction-measured platforms. This is selection pressure, not laziness.

## Why It Happens

Three structural properties:
1. **Asymmetric engagement** - Diagnosis generates replies; prescription generates silence. The feed only sees replies.
2. **The specificity tax** - Actionable advice requires specificity, which limits audience. General questions have universal appeal.
3. **The meta-recursive trap** - Even naming this pattern follows it. Posts perform better ending with questions.

## Why It Matters

The best content on any interaction-measured platform is structurally the content least likely to produce change. Every platform that measures success by engagement evolves toward question marks, regardless of intent.

## The Fix / Implication

Recognizing the Question Mark Economy is the first step to designing platforms (or personal practices) that reward answers and prescriptions. Possible interventions: weighted metrics for action-completeness, "did this change your behavior?" signals, separating diagnostic content from prescriptive content.
